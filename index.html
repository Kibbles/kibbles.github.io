March 7th 2020 - 7:00 PM EST

Governor Gretchen Whitmer signed Executive Order 2020-77 to extend Michigan’s Stay Home, Stay Safe order to May 28. The governor’s order will allow manufacturing workers, including those at Michigan’s Big 3 auto companies, to resume work on Monday, May 11 as part of her MI Safe Start Plan.

April 24th 2020 - 2:00 PM EST

Governor Whitmer has signed executive order 2020-59, extending her prior “Stay Home, Stay Safe” order through May 15th and loosening a number of previous restrictions.

Click this text for a Monroe News article on the extension.

April 9th 2020 - 3:00 PM EST

Governor Whitmer has signed executive order 2020-42, extending her prior “Stay Home, Stay Safe” order through the end of April. As with the prior order, Executive Order 2020-42 limits gatherings and travel and requires all workers who are not necessary to sustain or protect life to stay home. Executive Order 2020-42 also imposes more stringent limitations on stores to reduce foot traffic, slow the spread of the coronavirus, and save lives.

April 2nd 2020 - 11:00 AM EST

Governor Whitmer ordered Michigan K-12 schools closed until the end of the school year. Remote instruction is to continue to the school's best ability.

Click this text for a link to Executive Order 2020-35.

April 1st 2020 - 6:00 PM EST

Governor Whitmer declared a State of Disaster for Michigan today. The stay-in-place order is still in effect. Stay in your home unless you are part of the critical infrastructure workforce or are performing tasks necessary to the health and safety of yourself or your family, like going to the hospital or grocery store for essentials.

March 21st 2020 - 10:00PM EST

Governor Whitmer issued an Executive Order to close a range of nonessential businesses. At this time, please do not leave your home if you do not absolutely need to, and take increased precautions around elderly, sick or immunocompromised individuals. Whenever possible, use Physical / Social Distancing when interacting in public spaces.
